Case Study

Higher Education · UAE · Jan 2025–May 2026

Education & Employment
Observatory

Strategic labour market intelligence platform bridging graduate outcomes to UAE employment data — enabling a national higher education institution to align programme design with real-world workforce demand.

Problem

The institution lacked a systematic way to connect its graduate outcomes to the broader UAE labour market. Programme design decisions were made without visibility into which sectors were growing, which skills were in demand, or how graduates from specific programmes were faring in the employment market. Accreditation bodies and government stakeholders increasingly required evidence of graduate employability aligned to national workforce priorities — a demand the institution could not meet with its existing reporting infrastructure.

Approach

Led the design and build of a three-layer intelligence platform. The first layer ingested graduate outcomes data (employment status, sector, job role, time to employment) from the institution's alumni tracking system. The second layer integrated UAE labour market data to provide sector-level demand signals and growth projections. The third layer mapped institution-specific skills and programme outputs to market demand categories. Power Query transformations built the cross-layer join logic and resolved inconsistent occupational classifications. DAX measures computed employment rate by programme, time-to-employment distributions, sector alignment scores, and skills gap analysis. Delivered a five-page observatory report used in board-level strategic reviews and accreditation submissions.

Key Outcome

The observatory became the institution's primary strategic intelligence tool for programme planning — replacing anecdotal market awareness with data-driven alignment analysis. Sector demand signals informed decisions to expand capacity in high-growth disciplines and review under-subscribed programmes with low employment absorption. Skills gap analysis provided faculty with direct input into curriculum design priorities. The platform was referenced in an accreditation submission as evidence of the institution's commitment to graduate employability and labour market alignment.
