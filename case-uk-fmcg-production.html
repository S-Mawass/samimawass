Problem

Production output was recorded manually on paper forms at shift handover. Weekly tallies were compiled by supervisors in Excel, but the process was inconsistent and often a week behind. Production managers had no intra-shift visibility into output progress, could not compare performance across lines or product types, and had no way to track trends over time without significant manual effort. Target attainment conversations in morning meetings were based on memory, not data.

Approach

Designed a lightweight shift-level data entry template replacing manual paper forms — structured to feed directly into the Power BI data model. Built Power Query transformations for shift, line, and product type normalisation. Wrote DAX measures for output volume, target attainment percentage, period-over-period output comparison, product mix ratios, and line efficiency ranking. Delivered a report with: Daily Production Summary (shift-by-shift output vs. target), Line Performance Comparison, Product Mix Trends, and Weekly Output Trend analysis — optimised for large-screen display on the factory floor.

Key Outcome

Morning production meetings shifted from reconstruction exercises to data-led conversations. Production managers could identify underperforming lines and shift patterns within hours rather than days. Product mix trend analysis revealed seasonality in output composition that the team used to improve raw material planning. Target attainment visibility improved by 20% and the manual weekly tally process was eliminated entirely.
