About

From intern to enterprise BI — built one model at a time.

BI Developer at Cedars BC working in Power BI, DAX, SQL, and SSAS Tabular — now extending that foundation into Microsoft Fabric and analytics engineering.

Sami Mawass
BI Developer · Power BI, SQL & Microsoft Fabric

Where it started.

I joined Cedars BC in 2021 as an intern in Tripoli, Lebanon, fresh out of a Management Information Systems degree. The internship was structured Microsoft Data & AI training, but what mattered was that interns worked on live client projects. Six months in, I'd written SQL that real stakeholders depended on — and I'd found the work I wanted to do. Cedars BC offered me a full-time seat in March 2022.

The work since.

Four years later, that seat has turned into 18+ delivered engagements across 7 countries — government, higher education, FMCG, energy, and more — most of it for clients in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and Europe, delivered remotely from Lebanon.

The engagement that shaped me most was a reporting platform for a UAE higher-education institution serving ~25,000 students. The existing SSAS Tabular model technically worked, but nobody could follow it. I rebuilt it as a proper star schema, cut dataset refresh from ~30 minutes to ~6, set up production row-level security — and trained a business user to manage that security without me. That last part became my standard for every project since: if the client needs me forever, I built it wrong.

These days I also mentor Cedars BC's BI interns on Power BI, DAX, and data modelling — the same path I came through.

How I work.

Most reporting fails not because the data is wrong, but because the model is opaque — unclear grain, unnamed measures, no documentation. I build semantic layers a stakeholder can actually follow.

In practice that means three habits: name every measure like someone else will read it tomorrow, document the grain before writing the first line of DAX, and hand over access and knowledge — not just dashboards.

What's next.

I'm deliberately extending from BI delivery into analytics engineering — dbt, modular SQL, Python, and the Microsoft Fabric platform (I earned the DP-600 Fabric Analytics Engineer certification in 2025). I'm a project-first learner: I study 4–5 hours a day, and everything I learn ends up in something built, not just something read.
