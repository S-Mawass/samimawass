Case Study

Higher Education · UAE · Jan 2025–May 2026

Student Directory &
Demographics Intelligence

Power BI student demographics and directory platform for a UAE national higher education institution — consolidating enrolment, nationality, programme, and cohort data for ~25,700 students into a single searchable intelligence layer.

Problem

The institution's student data was distributed across twelve or more departmental lists, each maintained independently with different structures, naming conventions, and update frequencies. Institutional reporting — to accreditation bodies, government authorities, and senior leadership — required significant manual reconciliation effort. Ad-hoc demographic queries (e.g., "how many female students are in year 2 engineering?") could take days to answer. There was no authoritative, institution-wide student directory that could be trusted as a single source of truth.

Approach

Led the data integration and model design work. Consolidated student records from twelve source systems through Power Query transformations that reconciled student IDs, standardised naming conventions, and resolved duplicate records. Built a student dimension table with attributes for programme, college, year of study, nationality, gender, enrolment type, and status. Wrote DAX measures for enrolment counts by any combination of dimensions, cohort-level retention rates, nationality distribution analysis, and programme capacity utilisation. Delivered a four-page report: Institutional Enrolment Summary, Demographics Deep Dive, Programme Distribution, and a Student Directory view with dynamic search and filtering.

Key Outcome

Institutional reporting preparation time for accreditation submissions fell significantly — demographic breakdowns that previously required manual extraction across twelve lists became a one-click export. The Demographics Deep Dive view revealed enrolment concentration patterns that informed admission strategy discussions. Ad-hoc segmentation queries that previously took days were answered in minutes. The student directory became the institution's authoritative reference for all enrolment data within three months of go-live.
